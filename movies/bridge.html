Plot

A KILLER'S CONFESSION.

A DETECTIVE'S OBSESSION.

A RACE AGAINST TIME.

"Confession of Murder" is a thrilling South Korean crime film that dives into the mind of a notorious serial killer and the relentless pursuit of justice by a determined detective. The story unfolds when Lee Doo-seok publishes a memoir detailing his murders after the statute of limitations expires, sparking public outrage and media frenzy.

My POV

What captivated me most about this film was the intense cat-and-mouse game between Detective Choi Hyung-goo, who is haunted by his failure to catch the killer, and Lee Doo-seok, whose charismatic persona and detailed confessions mesmerize the nation. The plot is filled with unexpected twists and revelations that kept me guessing until the very end. The film's exploration of justice, revenge, and the blurred lines between right and wrong makes it a compelling and thought-provoking watch.

Can a killer truly escape justice?

And what happens when the past refuses to stay buried?
