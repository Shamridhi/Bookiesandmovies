Plot

A WOMAN'S ACT OF VIOLENCE.

A THERAPIST'S OBSESSION.

"The Silent Patient" is an enthralling psychological thriller that delves into the mind of Alicia Berenson, a famous painter who suddenly stops speaking after being accused of murdering her husband. The story is narrated by Theo Faber, a criminal psychotherapist who becomes obsessed with uncovering the truth behind Alicia's silence.

My POV

For me, the brilliance of this book lies in its intricate plot and the way it keeps you guessing until the very end. Theo's relentless pursuit to get Alicia to speak again leads him down a path filled with twists and turns, revealing shocking secrets along the way. The psychological depth and unexpected revelations make this a gripping read that I couldn't put down.

What drives a person to silence?

And what happens when that silence is broken?
