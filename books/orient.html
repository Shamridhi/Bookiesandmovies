Plot

A LUXURY TRAIN.

A BRUTAL MURDER.

A WORLD-RENOWNED DETECTIVE.

"Murder on the Orient Express" is a classic mystery novel by Agatha Christie, which has been adapted into several films and television shows. The story follows the brilliant Belgian detective Hercule Poirot as he embarks on a journey aboard the luxurious Orient Express. When a passenger is found murdered, Poirot must use his keen intellect and unparalleled detective skills to unravel the intricate web of lies and deceit surrounding the case.

My POV

For me, "Murder on the Orient Express" is a masterpiece of the mystery genre. The confined setting of the train creates a tense and claustrophobic atmosphere, heightening the suspense with every twist and turn. Each character is a potential suspect, and the complex interplay of their motives and alibis keeps you guessing until the very end. Poirot's methodical and insightful approach to solving the murder is captivating, showcasing Agatha Christie's genius for crafting intricate plots and memorable characters.

Who among the passengers has a motive for murder?

And can Poirot uncover the truth before the train reaches its destination?
